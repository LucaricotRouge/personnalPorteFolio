Pizza Oven Made Of Mycelium

As part of my innovation project in my first year of my Masters, I decided to do a resilient future project. My aim is to make a pizza oven entirely out of mycelium composite in an extreme temperature environment of around 300Â°C. The choice of mycelium was made mainly for its advantages of lightness and thermal insulation. However, as a bio-material, exposure to high temperatures can lead to deformation of its structure and fire resistance. This is why I have used two protocols that I will combine to reinforce the fire resistance and thermal stability of the mycelium composite in the inner layer of the oven that will be directly exposed to the heat source. The first protocol enables fireproof mycelium to be grown directly by integrating silicon directly into the internal structure of the fungal fibres as they grow. The second protocol uses glass microspheres and a deacetylation process to enhance the thermal stability and fire resistance of the mycelium. The oven will have a spherical shape and the design will be inspired by the HyphaL mycelium enclosure, winner of the 2024 green concept award:

Prototype

To build this oven I took advantage of the living nature of mycelium, which allowed the oven to grow directly in a plaster mould. This first layer will form the outer structure of the oven. The inner layer will be made of a composite of reinforced and fireproof mycelium thanks to the application of the two protocols mentioned above. Before designing the final version of the oven, we created a first prototype in mycelium (without chemical modification) of medium size compared to the final version to study the behaviour of the mycelium available in our laboratory against fire. I then made a plaster mould in which I grew the mycelium into a half-sphere shape. At the same time, I made another cube-shaped prototype, this time with a PLA mould, to study the influence of geometry on the mycelium's resistance to fire.
